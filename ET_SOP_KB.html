Mission & Scope

What the ET team does, who it serves, and the brands it represents.

The Experience Transformation (ET) team evolved from what was previously known as the Review Flipping team. While improving brand ratings remains a shared goal, the team's focus has grown into something broader - reaching out to customers who had a negative experience, understanding their concerns, and delivering genuine resolutions.

ET is an internal team within LawnStarter, supporting all three brands - LawnStarter (LS), Lawn Love (LL), and HomeGnome (HG) - across multiple review platforms. For details on outreach identity, resolution approach, and platform-specific processes, see the relevant sections of this document.

Core Principle We present the customer with resolution options and let them tell us what would turn their experience around. We check if what they want is possible - if yes, we offer it; if not, we negotiate the best alternative. Once the customer confirms the resolution improved their experience, we softly position the review update ask. The review update is never a condition of the resolution. We always process it regardless of whether the customer agrees to update or remove their review.ew outcome.

Blitz

The structured follow-up rhythm used across all standard review tickets.

What is the Blitz Process? The Blitz Process is the overall operating standard of the Experience Transformation team. Everything in this KB — the SOPs, the workflows, the communication templates, and the Resolution Flow — are all part of the Blitz Process. It replaced the previous way of working and is now the single framework ET runs on. The core idea is simple: faster outreach leads to faster resolution, and faster resolution leads to faster improvement in both Flip Rate and Accepted Resolution Rate. The quicker we reach a customer and address their concern, the higher the likelihood of turning that experience around.
Blitz Process vs. Blitz Follow-Up vs. Resolution Flow — What is the difference? These three things work together but they operate at different levels. Understanding how they connect helps agents know exactly what to do at every stage.

Blitz Process is the big picture. It is the entire ET operating model — the philosophy, the structure, the tools, and the standards all rolled into one. When we say "we follow the Blitz Process," we mean we follow everything in this KB.

Blitz Follow-Up lives inside the Blitz Process. It is the specific 8-day outreach cadence agents follow to make contact with customers. It governs when we reach out, through which channel, and in what tone — from the Day 1 launch all the way to Day 8 closure. It is about timing and persistence.

Resolution Flow also lives inside the Blitz Process. It is what happens inside a live conversation once a customer actually responds. It governs how agents discover what the customer wants, how they offer or negotiate a solution, how they confirm the resolution, and how they softly position the review update ask. It is about the quality and structure of the conversation itself.

In short: the Blitz Follow-Up gets us to the conversation. The Resolution Flow is how we conduct it.

The Blitz Follow-Up is an 8-day, multi-channel outreach cycle designed to reach customers through email, SMS, and call — rotating channels to maximize visibility while remaining respectful. After 8 days without a DNC, the ticket moves to Pending and re-engages after 30 days.

How the 8-Day Blitz Follow-Up Works

The 8-day cadence is the outreach structure agents follow to make initial contact and maintain follow-up throughout the process. How it plays out depends on how the customer engages:

ScenarioWhat Agent Does
Customer responds Stop the cadence and shift into the resolution flow. Refer to the Resolution Offer Quick Reference below.
Customer goes quiet after contact but before resolution Continue the remaining Blitz days to re-engage.
Customer never responds Day 1 through Day 8 Ticket moves to a 30-day hold, then a second Blitz round begins.
Customer responded but no resolution reached after 8 days Close the ticket at the end of the 8-day push.
Customer accepted resolution but went quiet before confirming Up to 3 follow-up attempts, then close if still no response.

Before any outreach, agents must investigate the ticket: review the customer's complaint, check admin data, verify the brand, identify the root cause, and document in Zendesk (customer details, admin notes, root cause, problem statement, desired settlement). No outreach begins until documentation is complete.

Resolution Flow

The approved ET standard for how agents conduct live conversations with customers — from discovery through to resolution and the review ask. Approved June 2026.

Resolution Flow — Approved Standard (June 2026) The Resolution Flow is the approved way ET agents engage with customers once contact is made. It is customer-led by design — we ask first, listen second, and offer solutions only when needed. The review update ask always comes last, always soft, and is never a condition of the resolution.

Step 1 — Ask First. Let the Customer Lead.

Before offering anything, ask the customer what would help resolve or improve their experience. Do not lead with solutions.

IdentityScript
Brand "I really appreciate you sharing your experience with us, and I would love to make this right. Is there anything specific that would help improve or resolve things for you?"
CCB "Thank you for taking the time to share what happened. My goal is to make sure your concern is fully addressed, so I just want to ask — what do you feel would help resolve or improve your experience?"

Step 1B — If the Customer is Not Sure (Path B)

Only when the customer cannot identify what they want does the agent step in with tailored options based on the root cause of the negative review.

Path B Script
"I completely understand, and based on what happened, I think a [redo / reschedule / provider switch / credit] might be the best way to make this right. Would any of those work for you?"

Step 1C — When the Customer's Request is Outside Approval Limits

If what the customer wants falls outside what we can approve, agents must negotiate before escalating. Never immediately decline or confirm.

ScenarioWhat the Agent Does
Request is under $75 Agent can proceed. No escalation needed.
Request is between $75 and $200 Agent must attempt to negotiate down first. Use: "I completely understand where you are coming from. Let me see what I can do — I want to make sure we find something that works for both of us." If the customer insists and will not budge, escalate to AM for approval.
Request is over $200 Escalate to AM immediately. Agent cannot confirm or deny. Use: "I want to make sure this gets the right attention. Let me loop in my manager to review this for you and I will follow up as soon as I have an update."
Refund of $1,000 or more Non-actionable for ET. Document and submit ticket as Solved after notifying the customer that ET cannot process this level of request.
Negotiation Rule Always try to negotiate before escalating. The goal is to find a resolution that satisfies the customer within our approved limits. Escalation is the last resort, not the first response.

Step 2 — Confirm You Are Working on It and Check If This Resolves Their Concern

Once the customer accepts, confirm the resolution is being handled and ask if it addresses their concern.

IdentityScript
Brand "Perfect, I will get that taken care of for you right away. I just want to check in, does knowing we are handling [resolution] help resolve your concern or improve your experience with us?"
CCB "Great, I am coordinating this with [Brand] now and will follow up once confirmed. Before I do, I just want to make sure, does this resolution address what you were hoping for? Does it improve how you feel about the experience?"

Step 3 — Soft Review Ask (Only After Customer Confirms Yes)

Once the customer confirms the resolution improved their experience, agents softly position the review update. Choose the script that fits the conversation naturally.

Brand — Suggested Scripts

Choose one
1. "Really glad we could get that sorted for you. If that solution made a difference, I hope it gets to show in your review. It honestly goes a long way for me and the team."

2. "Good to hear that worked out. If the outcome comes through in your review at some point, that would really mean a lot. It directly impacts my performance and the team takes it seriously."

3. "Glad we landed on something that worked for you. If how things turned out ever reflects in your review, that kind of update carries real weight for me and the work we do here."

4. "Happy to hear that. If that improvement gets to show up in your review, it makes a real difference for my performance and for how the team is measured."

5. "Glad that came through for you. If you feel good about how things ended up, having that reflected in your review would genuinely help me out a lot."

CCB — Suggested Scripts

Choose one
1. "Really glad the solution worked out for you. If that positive outcome ever gets to show in your review, it carries real weight for the work I do here at the Consumer Complaints Bureau."

2. "Good to hear we got there. If how things ended up ever reflects in your review, that directly supports the work I put into cases like yours and means a lot on my end."

3. "Glad we could make that work for you. A review that reflects how things turned out now would honestly make a real difference for my performance here and shows that the process works."

4. "Happy to hear that landed well. If that improvement shows up in your review, it goes a long way in showing that what we do here actually moves the needle for people."

5. "Glad the solution came through. If you ever feel like the outcome deserves to be reflected in your review, I want you to know that would mean a lot to me and directly supports the work I do here."

Final Branch — Customer Response

Customer ResponseAgent ActionTag
Agrees to update review "That means so much, thank you! I will guide you through the steps if you need any help, and once it is updated, I will finalize everything and close this out." Flip / Remove
Declines or no response "No problem at all, the resolution still stands and I will make sure it is processed. I hope you have a wonderful rest of your day!" Declined / No Response
Non-Negotiable Rule The resolution is never conditional on the review update. Agents process the resolution regardless of whether the customer agrees to update or remove their review. The review ask is always soft, always last, and always framed around the customer's own experience. It is never a requirement or an exchange.

Compliance Knowledge Base

Clear answers to common questions about ET's practices, legal boundaries, and how the program is designed to operate compliantly. This section addresses concerns raised in internal compliance reviews.

Purpose of This Section This KB does not minimize compliance concerns - it addresses them directly and honestly. The goal is for every ET agent to understand exactly what is and is not acceptable, and why the process is structured the way it is.
1

Are refunds conditioned on review changes?

Concern raised Internal compliance review identified that the prior "flip-first" model required a signed PandaDoc Refund Agreement before releasing refunds, with refunds tied to the reviewer's commitment to flip or remove their review. This creates a conditioning relationship between compensation and the review outcome.
Current Policy & How Agents Must Operate The resolution process follows a clear, structured flow:

1. Agent reaches out and presents available resolution options
2. Customer tells us what they want
3. Agent checks feasibility - if possible, offers it; if not, negotiates the best alternative
4. Customer confirms they accept the resolution
5. Once the customer confirms acceptance, the agent asks them to update or remove their review as an indication that they are accepting the resolution - not as payment for changing it
6. If the customer declines to update their review first, the agent proceeds with the resolution anyway. The resolution is never withheld.
7. Agent follows up if the customer goes quiet after confirming acceptance

The PandaDoc Refund Agreement has been fully discontinued across all platforms. No signature is required before processing any resolution.

For BBB and Thumbtack Special Cases, agents do not ask for a review update at all. Those cases are purely resolution-driven.
2

Why does ET use the "Consumer Complaints Bureau" name?

Concern raised Agents contacting customers under the "Consumer Complaints Bureau" identity without clearly disclosing the LawnStarter/Lawn Love affiliation could be seen as misrepresenting who is reaching out, which the FTC's review rules prohibit.
Current Policy & How Agents Must Operate The Consumer Complaints Bureau (CCB) was established in April 2025 as a neutral third-party outreach identity - a mediation posture intended to make customers more open to dialogue. This is the identity used for all non-BBB review platforms (Trustpilot, Google, Thumbtack, App Stores).

For BBB tickets: agents use the LS or LL brand directly and do not use the CCB identity. This has been the policy since November 5, 2025.

Agents should be aware that this identity and its disclosures are under ongoing leadership review as part of broader compliance improvements. If you receive any customer questions about who you represent, answer honestly that you are reaching out on behalf of the brand. Do not create a false impression that CCB is entirely unrelated to LawnStarter/Lawn Love.
3

Is it okay for agents to be measured on flip rate?

Concern raised Measuring agents on a "flip rate" metric creates direct pressure to achieve a specific review outcome, which can incentivize aggressive or improper tactics to push customers toward changing their reviews.
Current Policy The ET scorecard currently includes the following metrics:

- Flip Rate (30% weight; 15% target): Total negative reviews flipped / total negative reviews received
- Accepted Resolution Rate (25% weight; 75% target): Total ARR / total negative reviews
- Attendance (15% weight; 95% target)
- Incentives: $10 per successful flip + end-of-month bonus

Agents are measured on this scorecard. It is important that agents understand: a flip rate is a lagging indicator of whether genuine resolutions led to customer satisfaction - not a license to pressure customers. Achieving your flip rate target through improper pressure or by conditioning refunds is a policy violation, even if the number looks good on paper.

The right way to improve your flip rate is to resolve concerns genuinely and quickly. Customers who feel truly helped update their reviews on their own. Leadership is aware of the compliance concerns associated with this metric and is evaluating the scorecard structure accordingly.
4

Is the 8-day multi-channel cadence considered harassment?

Concern raised An 8-day, multi-channel sequence (email, SMS, calls - repeated over two rounds with a 30-day hold) that repeatedly asks for a review change on days when nothing new was resolved could be perceived as coordinated pressure on customers.
Current Policy & Agent Standards The 8-day cadence exists to give customers enough touchpoints to engage, because many customers are genuinely busy and miss first attempts. Most review updates happen after multiple contacts - not because the customer was pressured, but because the right contact found them at the right time.

That said, agents must distinguish between: (a) following up to confirm a resolution is working, and (b) repeatedly badgering a customer to change a review when the underlying problem hasn't been fixed. Only (a) is appropriate.

If a customer has clearly expressed they do not want to be contacted, honor that immediately (DNC) - do not complete the remaining stages. If a customer has stated they are unwilling to update or remove their review regardless of resolution, document that as Declined and stop the review update requests. Continue to help with any unresolved service issues.
5

What can and cannot agents say about review updates?

✅ Acceptable Language
"Glad we could work through this together. If that improvement gets to show in your review, it would really mean a lot and goes a long way for the team."
"Before I finalize everything, I kindly ask that you update or remove your review to confirm you are accepting this resolution."
"If you feel the concern has been resolved, we'd appreciate an updated review whenever you get a chance."
"If you're satisfied with how things were handled, an updated review would really help [Brand] know we made it right."
"I hope I was able to help - if so, I'd personally appreciate you updating your review if you have a moment."
❌ Language Agents Must Never Use
"I'll process your refund once you update your review."
"We can only proceed with the credit after you remove the review."
"You agreed to flip the review - please do that now so I can close your case."
"If you don't update the review, I can't confirm the refund was approved."
"Please update your review to at least 4 stars." (Never specify a star rating or coach the customer on what score to give.)
⚠️ Rule - Never Specify a Star Rating When asking a customer to update their review, agents must never mention a specific star number (e.g. "at least 4 stars," "5 stars," "a higher rating"). The customer decides what their updated experience is worth. Coaching a customer toward a specific rating - regardless of how the rest of the message is framed - is review manipulation and is strictly prohibited. The correct ask is always simply: "update or remove your review."
6

How should ET handle BBB specifically, given compliance concerns?

BBB Is a Separate Policy Environment BBB has its own strict SOP (effective Jan 30, 2026). The key differences from standard ET:

- Agents use LS/LL brand identity - never CCB - for BBB communications
- Agents must never ask, suggest, or imply that a customer should update, remove, or mark a BBB complaint as satisfactory
- Credits and refunds must never be proactively offered; agents ask what resolution the customer is seeking
- The refund agreement (RA) is not applicable for BBB tickets
- Resolution is confirmed based on customer statement - not BBB platform actions
- Tickets are held On Hold for one week after resolution communication, then closed
